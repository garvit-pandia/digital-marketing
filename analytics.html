Web Analytics

The measurement, collection, analysis, and reporting of web data. Analytics is not about looking at graphs; it's about finding actionable insights to optimize user experience and business conversion.

Advanced Analysis Concepts

Attribution Models

Who gets credit for the sale?

  • Last Click: The final touchpoint gets 100% credit. (Default, but biased).
  • First Click: The channel that introduced the user gets credit.
  • Linear: Everyone shares equal credit.
  • Data-Driven: AI assigns credit based on actual impact.

Cohort Analysis

Grouping users by shared characteristics (usually time).

Example: analyzing the retention rate of users who signed up in January vs. users who signed up in February. This helps isolate whether product changes improved retention.

Privacy & Compliance

The changing landscape of data.

  • GDPR/CCPA: User consent is mandatory.
  • Cookie-less Future: Moving towards server-side tracking and first-party data collection as third-party cookies retire.

The ABCs of Analytics

Acquisition: How users find you (Organic, Paid, Social).

Behavior: What they do (Pages/Session, Avg. Time, Event Clicks).

Conversion: Did they effectuate a goal? (Purchase, Sign Up, Download).

Tools of the Trade

Google Analytics 4 (GA4): Event-based tracking standard.

Google Tag Manager (GTM): Managing tracking codes without dev help.

Hotjar/CrazyEgg: Heatmaps and screen recordings for UX.

Mixpanel/Amplitude: Product analytics for apps.

The Analysis Funnel

  1. Acquisition: Bringing people in.
  2. Activation: The "Aha!" moment.
  3. Retention: Keeping them engaged.
  4. Referral: Turning them into advocates.
  5. Revenue: Monetization.

Data vs. Insight

Data is just numbers. Insight is the "Why" behind the numbers. Don't report "Traffic is up 10%". Report "Traffic is up 10% because our new LinkedIn campaign resonated with the C-Suite audience, resulting in higher quality leads."
