Affiliate Marketing

A performance-based marketing strategy where an individual or business earns a commission for promoting another company's product or service. The affiliate searches for a product they enjoy, promotes it to others, and earns a piece of the profit for each sale that they make.

Three Core Players

1. The Merchant

Also known as the seller, brand, or vendor.

They create the product and handle fulfillment. They don't need to be actively involved in marketing, as they have affiliates doing it for them.

2. The Affiliate

Also known as the publisher.

They market the product to their audience. If the consumer ends up buying the product, the affiliate receives a portion of the revenue.

3. The Consumer

The driver of the system.

They learn about the product through the affiliate's content. They may or may not know they are part of an affiliate system.

Finding a Niche

Success comes from specialization.

  • Passion: Something you care about.
  • Profitability: Are people spending money here?
  • Competition: Can you offer a unique angle?

Legal Requirements

Stay compliant.

  • FTC Disclosure: You MUST disclose that you earn a commission. It should be "Clear and Conspicuous".
  • Cookie Duration: How long does the tracking last? (24 hours vs 30 days).

Promotion Channels

Review Sites: "Best toaster 2024".

Comparison Sites: Price comparisons.

Influencers: Unboxing videos.

Email Lists: Curated recommendations.

The Affiliate Types

Unattached

PPC campaigns where you have no connection to the product or audience.

Related

Promoting products related to your niche, but you don't necessarily use them.

Involved

You use the product, believe in it, and recommend it personally.

Trust is Currency

For affiliate marketing to sustainable, transparency is key. Audiences buy from affiliates they trust. Disclosing affiliate relationships is not only a legal requirement in many regions but also builds credibility.
