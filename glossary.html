Marketing Glossary

A comprehensive A-Z list of digital marketing terms. Understanding the lingo is the first step to mastering the craft.

A - E

A/B Testing
A method of comparing two versions of a webpage or app against each other to determine which one performs better. Also known as split testing.
Affiliate Marketing
A performance-based marketing arrangement where a retailer pays a commission to an external website for traffic or sales generated from its referrals.
Algorithm
A set of rules available in computer programs that searches deliver relevant results to users. In social media, it determines what content you see.
Bounce Rate
The percentage of visitors to a particular website who navigate away from the site after viewing only one page. A high bounce rate often indicates irrelevant content or bad UX.
Buyer Persona
A semi-fictional representation of your ideal customer based on market research and real data about your existing customers.
CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)
The cost associated with convincing a customer to buy a product/service. Calculated by dividing all the costs spent on acquiring more customers (marketing expenses) by the number of customers acquired.
CTR (Click-Through Rate)
The ratio of users who click on a specific link to the number of total users who view a page, email, or advertisement. High CTR means your creative is resonating.
Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO)
The process of increasing the percentage of website visitors who take a desired action.

F - O

Funnel
The set of steps a visitor needs to go through before they can reach the conversion. (Awareness -> Interest -> Desire -> Action).
Impressions
The number of times a piece of content is displayed, no matter if it was clicked or not.
KPI (Key Performance Indicator)
A measurable value that demonstrates how effectively a company is achieving key business objectives.
Lead Magnet
A free item or service that is given away for the purpose of gathering contact details; for example, lead magnets can be trial subscriptions, samples, white papers, e-newsletters, and free consultations.
Organic Traffic
Visitors who land on your website from unpaid sources, utilizing search engines like Google or Bing. This is the "holy grail" of long-term marketing.

P - Z

PPC (Pay-Per-Click)
An internet advertising model used to drive traffic to websites, in which an advertiser pays a publisher when the ad is clicked.
ROI (Return on Investment)
A performance measure used to evaluate the efficiency or profitability of an investment. (Net Profit / Cost of Investment) x 100.
Retargeting
A form of online advertising that can help you keep your brand in front of bounced traffic after they leave your website.
SEO (Search Engine Optimization)
The practice of optimizing a website to improve its visibility and ranking in search engine results pages.
UGC (User Generated Content)
Any form of content, such as images, videos, text, and audio, that has been posted by users on online platforms such as social media and wikis.
