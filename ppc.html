PPC Advertising

Pay-Per-Click (PPC) is an online advertising model where advertisers pay a fee each time one of their ads is clicked. It's essentially a way of buying visits to your site, rather than attempting to "earn" those visits organically.

Google Ads Mechanics

Quality Score

It's not just about the highest bid.

  • Ad Relevance: Does the ad match the keyword?
  • Expected CTR: How likely is it to be clicked?
  • Landing Page Experience: Is it relevant and fast?

Targeting Options

Precision is profit.

  • Keywords: Phrase match, Broad match, Exact match.
  • Demographics: Audiences by age, gender, income.
  • Negative Keywords: Terms you don't want to show for (e.g., "free").

Remarketing

The "Stalker" Ads (done ethically).

  • Concept: Showing ads to people who visited your site but didn't convert.
  • Dynamic Remarketing: Showing the exact product they viewed.
  • Video Remarketing: YouTube ads for past viewers.

PPC Platforms

Google Ads: High intent search traffic.

Facebook/Instagram Ads: Visual, interest-based discovery.

LinkedIn Ads: High-value B2B decision makers.

Ad Extensions

Make your ad bigger for free.

  • Sitelink Extensions: Links to specific pages.
  • Callout Extensions: "Free Shipping", "24/7 Support".
  • Structured Snippets: "Styles: Men's, Women's, Kids".

Bidding Strategies

Manual CPC: You control the max bid.

Maximize Clicks: Get as much traffic as possible.

Target CPA: Get conversions at a specific cost.

Target ROAS: Return On Ad Spend goal.

PPC vs. SEO

PPC buys you a seat at the table immediately. SEO earns you a seat over time. The best strategy uses both: PPC for immediate data and sales, and SEO for long-term brand building and reduced costs.

PPC vs SEO

Speed: PPC is instant; SEO takes months.

Cost: PPC requires continuous budget; SEO has upfront effort but long-term payoff.

Control: PPC offers precise control over message and targeting.
